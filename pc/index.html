Build Highlights

🎮

RTX 3070

The heart of this build. Ray tracing, DLSS, 1440p gaming at high settings — this card handles everything I throw at it. Two GPUs because why not have a backup.

🧠

Ryzen 5 5600

Six cores of pure efficiency. Paired with the Hyper 212 cooler, temps stay under control even during long gaming sessions. The AM4 platform that keeps on giving.

💾

32GB RAM

Two different kits running in harmony. 32 gigs total means I can have Chrome, games, Discord, and OBS all running without worrying about memory.

🌈

Full ARGB

The CMP520 case with ARGB fans, the Spectrum V3 cooler, the RAM RGB — everything syncs up. Because a PC without RGB is just a box with wires.

Storage Strategy

The WD Blue SN550 NVMe handles the OS and frequently used applications. Windows boots in seconds, and games on the SSD have virtually no load times. It's a night-and-day difference from the old hard drive days.

The 1TB WD Blue HDD is the workhorse for bulk storage — games I don't play as often, downloads, backups, and media files. Having that extra terabyte means I never have to worry about running out of space.

I've been thinking about adding another NVMe drive for a dedicated game library, but for now this combo works perfectly. Fast where it matters, spacious where it counts.

The Build Journey

This PC wasn't built in a day. It started as a basic Ryzen build and evolved over time. The GPU was the biggest investment — finding an RTX 3070 at a reasonable price during the shortage era was its own adventure.

Cable management took three attempts to get right. The first time looked like a bird's nest behind the motherboard tray. Now it's clean enough that I'm not embarrassed to show the back panel.

Every component was researched, compared, and deliberated over. Hours of YouTube reviews, Reddit threads, and benchmark spreadsheets went into each decision. That's the beauty of building your own — every part means something.

The Cooler Master CMP520 case was chosen for airflow and aesthetics. The mesh front panel keeps everything cool, and the tempered glass side panel lets the ARGB do its thing. Form meets function.

Built with obsession. No frameworks. Just vibes.
