My Phone

It gets the job done. Mostly.

Second hand phone 💸💸

The Story

Look, not everyone needs the latest flagship with a 200MP camera and a titanium frame. Some of us are perfectly happy with a phone that makes calls, runs WhatsApp, and doesn't die before noon.

This phone was bought second hand. It's got character. A few scratches here and there that tell stories. The battery life is... creative. Let's just say I've become really good at finding charging ports in public places.

Does it run games? Technically yes. Does it run them well? Let's not talk about that. But for browsing, messaging, and the occasional doom-scroll through social media, it does the job surprisingly well.

Phone Philosophy

💰

Budget Friendly

Why spend a lakh on a phone when you can spend that on PC parts? Priorities. The saved money went straight into RGB fans and an RTX 3070.

🛡️

Battle Tested

This phone has survived drops, rain, and being sat on. It's basically indestructible at this point. The screen protector is doing god's work.

📸

Camera? Sure.

The camera exists. It takes photos. Are they award-winning? No. But they capture memories, and that's what matters... right?

🔄

Sustainability

Buying second hand is environmentally conscious. I'm basically saving the planet one used phone at a time. You're welcome, Earth.

Real Talk

The truth is, I spend 90% of my time on my PC anyway. The phone is just for when I'm away from my desk, which honestly isn't that often. It handles notifications, quick replies, and the occasional phone call (yes, people still make those).

I've thought about upgrading, but every time I look at flagship prices, I think about what PC components I could buy instead. A new phone or another 32GB of RAM? The choice is obvious.

Maybe one day I'll get something nicer. But for now, this trusty second-hand warrior keeps going strong. And honestly? There's something satisfying about that.

Built with obsession. No frameworks. Just vibes.
